Biography

The Man Behind
The Lens

Born in the Bronx, New York in 1928, Garry Winogrand became one of the most influential photographers of the twentieth century. Armed with a Leica and an insatiable hunger for life's fleeting moments, he roamed the streets of America documenting everything from Times Square to Texas rodeos.

He studied painting at City College of New York before turning to photography, studying under Alexey Brodovitch. His work captured the frenetic, optimistic, and sometimes unsettling energy of postwar America with unmatched vitality.

When he died in 1984, he left behind over 2,500 undeveloped rolls of film — a final testament to his belief that the act of photographing was itself the purpose.
